Geography

Comprising 7,641 islands in Southeast Asia situated in the western Pacific Ocean, the Philippines boasts a distinctive and varied topography.

These islands are divided into three main regions: Mindanao in the south, Visayas in the center, and Luzon in the north.

Due to its location on the Pacific Ring of Fire, which makes it vulnerable to earthquakes and volcanic eruptions, the nation is home to a variety of mountains, rainforests, fertile plains, rivers, and hundreds of volcanoes, including famous ones like Mayon and Taal.

In addition to a rainy season and frequent typhoons, its tropical climate offers warm weather all year round.

The Philippines is one of the most ecologically diverse countries in the world because of its vast coastlines, which include beaches, coral reefs, and a wealth of marine life.

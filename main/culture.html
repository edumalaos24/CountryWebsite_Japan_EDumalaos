Culture

Spanish colonial rule, American presence, and centuries of contact with Asian neighbors have all contributed to the Philippines' vibrant blend of indigenous customs and foreign influences.

Family, community, and hospitality are highly valued in Filipino society, as evidenced by customs like pakikisama (harmonious relations) and bayanihan (communal unity).

Everyday life and festivities are heavily influenced by religion, particularly Catholicism, which hosts vibrant fiestas all year long to honor patron saints.

Filipino culture is also artistic and expressive, as evidenced by modern music and film, traditional weaving, and folk dances like tinikling.

Local flavors are showcased in dishes like lechon, sinigang, and adobo, which are important components of cultural identity.

Filipinos have a strong sense of resiliency, humor, and pride in their heritage despite their diversity, which includes over 170 languages and numerous regional customs.
